Kapurthala, often referred to as the "Paris of Punjab", is a historic city known for its elegant French-inspired architecture, royal heritage, and beautiful landmarks. Once the capital of the princely state of Kapurthala, the city reflects the vision of Maharaja Jagatjit Singh, who admired European art, culture, and architecture.

Why is it called the Paris of Punjab?

The city's majestic buildings like the Jagatjit Palace and the Moorish Mosque were designed in European styles, making Kapurthala resemble a French city. The well-planned gardens, boulevards, and monuments reflect a blend of Indo-European culture, which makes this city truly unique in Punjab.
