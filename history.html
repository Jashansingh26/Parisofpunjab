Kapurthala‚Äôs rich and royal history dates back to the 11th century. It was once ruled by the Ahluwalia dynasty and later became the capital of a princely state under British India.

The city flourished under Maharaja Jagatjit Singh, who ruled from the late 19th century into the early 20th century. A great admirer of French culture, he transformed Kapurthala into a city of European elegance.

During his reign, many grand architectural projects were undertaken, such as Jagatjit Palace (modeled after Versailles in France), the Moorish Mosque, and the State Gurudwara.

After India gained independence, Kapurthala merged into the Indian Union and became part of Punjab. Today, its legacy lives on through its heritage buildings, cultural richness, and contributions to modern Punjab.
